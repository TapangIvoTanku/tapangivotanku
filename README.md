## About Me

I build AI systems that operate at Fortune 5 scale by day — and study how those same systems are reshaping the strategic logic of international conflict by night.

My research asks a deceptively simple question: **can a machine bluff?**

I study how artificial intelligence reshapes the strategic landscape of international security. My work sits at the intersection of **crisis bargaining theory**, **generative AI**, and **computational social science** — using large language models, game-theoretic simulation, and NLP to model the strategic behavior of states under conditions of uncertainty and incomplete information.

**Research Interests:** AI & International Security · Crisis Bargaining · Deterrence Theory · Generative AI · Computational Political Science · LLM Alignment

## 🔬 Featured Research

### [The Bluffing Machine: Large Language Models as Strategic Deceivers in Crisis Bargaining](https://github.com/TapangIvoTanku/the-bluffing-machine)

The first formal experimental test of whether Large Language Models can engage in **strategic deception** in a crisis bargaining context. Tests three frontier LLMs across 900 simulated crisis interactions using the **Signal-Reason-Outcome (S-R-O)** qualitative coding framework.
